About

I am a current MSc Geographical Information Systems (GIS) student at the University of Leeds (2019-2020). Modules chosen comprise of Web-based GIS, Geodemographics and Neighbourhood Analysis, Data Visualisation and Analysis, Programming for Geographical Information Analysis: Core Skills and Digital Image Processing for Environmental Remote Sensing. A Personal and Professional Development project involved "Identification of Informal Settlements across the city of Buenos Aires, Argentina using ENVI Rule-Based Classification". This course covered the use of ArcMap, QGIS, SPSS, Tableau, R and Erdas Imagine.

Current work includes a 15,000 word Dissertation entitled "Digital Divide: Mapping the Geodemographics of Internet Accessibility across Great Britain".

I was previously awarded a first class BSc Geography degree. My dissertation focussed on "Potentially Harmful Elements due to Metal Mining contamination in Cononley Beck, a tributary of the River Aire, North Yorkshire". All work can be made available upon request. I completed this alongside six modules including an advanced GIS and Remote Sensing course, where 92% was awarded upon course completion. This course covered the use of ArcMap and extensions: Model Builder and Network Analyst alongside ArcGlobe and ArcScene.

Upon graduating I am hoping to enter the Geospatial sector.
